1.f Construction and Use of Electromagnets

The Electromagnet

An electromagnet consists of three main parts

    A Coil. The coil is made from turns of INSULATED copper wire.

    A Core. The coil is wound on a core of soft magnetic material such as Iron.

    A Battery or Power Supply. This is needed to provide current for the coil.

An important property of an electromagnet is that it loses its magnetic effect as soon as the electric current is switched off.

--------------------

The Stength of an Electromagnet

The strength of an Electromagnet depends upon

    1. The size of the current.

    2. The number of turns the coil has.

    3. The material that the core is made from.

------------------

Uses of Electromagnets

The Electric Bell

1. When the switch is closed an electric current flows through the electromagnet.

2. The iron bar next to it is attracted. (It becomes an induced magnet)

3. The hammer hits the bell and at the same time the circuit is broken when the contacts move apart.

4. The electromagnet switches off and the steel spring makes the iron bar move back. The contacts close and the whole cycle starts again.

The Relay

1. When the switch is closed a current flows through the electromagnet.

2. The iron bar is attracted to the electromagnet.

3. The contacts are closed in the high current circuit.

A small current is used to control a large current.

This sort of circuit is used to control the starter motor in a car.
